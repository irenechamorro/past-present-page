PAST IS PRESENT

The purpose of this project was to emulate the style of a chosen past designer, in

this case William Morris, and modernize it. In this group project we made a series of

designed objects and a setting that was stylized with modern techniques, while keeping

aspects of Art Nouveau. I had the role of creating a wallpaper and design that would

be applied to different objects, as well as creating an ad for the fictional company

that would sell these objects.

To keep in line with the Art Nouveau style I chose to draw decorative, thin flowers

in very neutral colors, as William Morris used. The modernity comes out in the placement

of the large repetitive print that is often used in minimalist interior design that has

been very popular in recent years.

This teacup set is an example of the kind of products this fusion design company would

sell. The print of the cup and plate are floral and delicate, whereas the form of the

product is very simple and uncomplicated, with the cup possibly being made of plastic

or another mordern material used to make everyday tableware.

The poster was made to encapsulate the style of the company, Ornaments and Elgance, and

was used to advertize a fictional exhibition of the products we created with the William

Morris style in mind. The poster itself has a delicate flowery design around the text

banner, and is a beige color that displays age and wealth, appealing to an audience that

would enjoy modernized Art Nouveau products. The font choice was the most difficult part

of this project, as I wanted to keep in mind all the different style elements at play. I

tried drawing out the lettering, and then experimented with some existing fonts. Eventually,

I used a combination of those two techniques and finished with the poster above.
